Hi Y'all! I'm Michelle!

Born and raised in Texas, I come from Fort Worth, attended UNT in Denton, and then moved to Dallas. DFW is my home and I haven't known anything else. I received a bachelor's degree from the University of North Texas in Integrative Studies. I worked for years in the Hospitality Industry until the pandemic hit. When the world came to a stop, I realized my career had no work-life balance and I needed a change.

That's when I decided to make a career change and enter into the world of Software Development. I started teaching myself how to code through free online programs such as Kahn Academy & Codecademy. My first project was an html/css baby shower website for my sister's baby shower (it was virtual). I created a portfolio website for myself and worked with a mentor on a side project. I decided to enroll at Dallas College to continue my education in a more structured format.

Courses taken at Dallas College

I enrolled in a Advanced Web Applciation Development Certificate. Since I already have an (unrelated) bachelor's degree, I didn't think I could afford to pursue another one, especially while unemployed from the pandemic. In my time at Dallas College, I have taken Java and Advanced Java programming, Intermediate and Advanced Web Programming, Database Programming, Web Design 1, and Linux Installation/Configuration. The web programming courses were a lot of HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I'm currently taking Web Authoring, Programming Logic & Design, and a Comprehensive Software Project. After this semester, I'm just missing a Mobile Applicaiton Development course and I will have completed my certificate!

Professional Goals

My end goal is to be a talented Software Engineer/Developer doing work toward something I'm interested in. In Summer 2022, I managed to land my first entry level software developer job at an Education Technology company. I'm very grateful for my job and all the learning I'm able to do but I want to do more. Hopefully after I complete my certificate, I can enroll in a Master's program for Computer Science. There are a few online Master's programs that don't require my bachelor's to be in computer science. However, they are very competitive to get into. I hope to do some side projects to build up my portfolio so I can get into one of these programs. I have some ideas for projects but am not 100% sure how to execute them. The courses I'm currently taking will hopefully give me some insite into starting a project on my own.

If I'm able to enroll in a Master's program, I'm hoping I'll be able to further my knowledge to help me perform better at my current company. I would like to one day be knowledgeable enough to mentor someone else who is new and starting out like I am now. I eventually would like to move to a larger company where there is more opportunity or even maybe start a company of my own one day with what I learn.
